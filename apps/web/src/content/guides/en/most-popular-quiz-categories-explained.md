FunSona organizes its quizzes into eight main categories, visible on the [Explore](/en/explore) page. If you're new to the platform, it might not be obvious which category to check out — or, if you create quizzes, which one your quiz belongs in. This guide walks through each one, explaining what you'll actually find there and who it was built for.

## Personality

The [Personality](/en/explore/personality) category is home to FunSona's most popular quizzes: tests that connect your everyday choices to archetypes, energies, and personality traits to reveal a result you'll genuinely recognize yourself in. They range from classics like "which zodiac sign really matches you" to more creative formats, like discovering your aura, your element, or which character from a specific universe matches your vibe. Results usually come with an explanation of why that trait matches your answers — so besides playing, you learn something about yourself. It's the ideal category for a quick answer session and comparing results with friends.

## Trivia

The [Trivia](/en/explore/trivia) category is for people who want to test how much they actually know about a subject, rather than discover a personality result. Questions here have a right answer: every quiz presents multiple-choice questions about pop culture, history, science, geography, or trivia about a specific fandom, and at the end you get a score showing how many you got right. It's the perfect category for competing with friends over who scores highest, or just learning something new while playing.

## Love

The [Love](/en/explore/love) category brings quizzes about relationships, compatibility, crushes, and the romantic (or not-so-romantic) side of life. These are tests meant to be played solo, sent to a crush as a hint, or answered together with a partner to see if the answers match. The tone is always light — no result here should be taken as serious relationship advice, it's pure entertainment meant to spark recognition and good conversations.

## Games

The [Games](/en/explore/games) category is home to quizzes for anyone who grew up (or still lives) inside video games: tests about which character from a game matches your personality, knowledge quizzes about entire franchises, and even nostalgic tests about consoles and gaming eras. The idea is to blend gamer culture with self-discovery, understanding your play style — free exploration or direct missions, competitive multiplayer or solo campaigns — and connecting that to a result within the gaming universe.

## Movies

The [Movies](/en/explore/movies) category is dedicated to everything cinema: memorable characters, entire franchises, genres, and the kind of trivia only true movie buffs really know. Quizzes range from discovering which character matches you to testing how much you actually know about a specific movie, director, decade, or genre. There's room for both recent blockbusters and cult classics.

## Music

The [Music](/en/explore/music) category is for anyone who lives with headphones cranked up: tests that connect your musical taste to your personality, and quizzes aimed at fans of specific bands and artists, testing knowledge about discography and career trivia. The goal is to capture the vibe and energy music brings out in you, delivering a result that works almost like a personal soundtrack.

## TV

The [TV](/en/explore/tv) category is the space for anyone who binges shows, follows soap operas, or has a TV program as part of their routine. Quizzes range from discovering which character from a show matches you to knowledge tests about episodes, plots, and details only close watchers would remember. Each quiz is built around specific elements of that universe, rather than generic questions.

## Knowledge

The [Knowledge](/en/explore/knowledge) category gathers general-knowledge quizzes for people who like learning while playing: history, science, geography, basic math, and everyday trivia, organized into multiple-choice questions with a right answer. It works well as a light review tool for students and as a general-knowledge challenge for the curious.

## How to pick the right category for your quiz

If you're creating a quiz and aren't sure which category it belongs in, the simplest question is: does your quiz have a right answer, or does it reveal a result about the person? If it has a right answer, it probably belongs in Trivia or Knowledge, depending on whether the topic is very specific (a movie, a franchise) or more general (general knowledge, science). If it reveals a result, the quiz's topic usually points to the right category — a quiz about which movie character matches you goes in Movies, one about romantic compatibility goes in Love, and so on.

Categorizing correctly helps other people find your quiz when they're browsing a specific topic, so it's worth choosing carefully instead of defaulting to the same category out of habit.
